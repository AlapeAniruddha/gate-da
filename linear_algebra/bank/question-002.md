What is the maximum rank that a $4 \times 8$​ matrix can have?

::: {.callout-note title="Hint" collapse=true}

The rank is the dimension of the row space. It is also equal to the dimension of the column space.

:::

::: {.callout-note title="Answer" collapse=true}

$4$

:::

::: {.callout-note title="Solution" collapse=true}

The row rank is equal to the column rank for any matrix. The row rank is the dimension of the row space. This can be at most $n$. The column rank is the dimension of the column space. This can be at most $m$. Therefore, the rank of a $m \times n$ matrix is at most $\min(m, n)$​.

:::
